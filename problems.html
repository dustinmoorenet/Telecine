Problems
        When I stated that the system works, I was not lying, but I have decided not to use it to transfer my film collection for several reasons. First, the scanner I bought for the system has given me nothing but trouble. I have to automate the scan operation using keystroke micros, because HP does not open up the scanner's full capabilities to TWAIN support. I can scan, but I can not turn on the transparent media adapter. Also, the scans of the film are too dark. I can fix this by cheating the light calibration before the scan, but it does not produce consistent results. Finally, the scanner was not designed to be used as much as I need it to be, and occasionally, the scanner jams as it makes a pass. The first problem greatly complicates and slows down scanning and the last two cause an unpredictability that I can not accept.
        The second reason the system will not be used is that the process of scanning and moving the film requires that the film be dragged across glass and plastic. This might damage the film. I tried to devise a system that didn't require as much contact, but the designs only complicated the system further.
